Dr. Sanaullah Kalwar

MBBS, FCPS (Gastroenterology)

Consultant Gastroenterologist | Sukkur, Sindh

As a gastroenterologist, my role is early detection and diagnosis of gastrointestinal (GI) cancers through advanced procedures, ensuring timely referrals to oncologists for life-saving treatment.

How I Help Diagnose GI Cancers

1. Endoscopy & Biopsy:

  • Oesophageal & Gastric Cancers: Directly visualize abnormal growths in the oesophagus/stomach via endoscopy and take tissue samples (biopsy) to confirm cancer.
  • Colon Cancer: Detect and remove precancerous polyps during colonoscopy, preventing progression to cancer.

2. ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography):

  • Pancreatic & Cholangiocarcinoma (Bile Duct) Cancers: Use ERCP to examine bile/pancreatic ducts, identify blockages/tumors, and collect biopsies or place stents to relieve symptoms.

3. Liver Disease & Cancer Screening:

  • Hepatocellular Carcinoma (Liver Cancer): Monitor high-risk patients (e.g., cirrhosis, hepatitis B/C) with ultrasound, blood tests, and biopsy to catch cancer early.

Why Early Diagnosis Matters

GI cancers often show no symptoms until advanced stages. My role is to:

  • Spot subtle warning signs (e.g., weight loss, jaundice, bleeding)
  • Use targeted tests to confirm cancer quickly
  • Refer you promptly to a medical oncologist (cancer specialist) to start chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or other treatments

Working Together for Better Outcomes

  • Team Approach: I collaborate closely with oncologists, surgeons, and radiologists to ensure seamless care.
  • Your Next Steps: If cancer is diagnosed, I guide you to trusted specialists and explain your diagnosis in simple terms.

Early Detection Saves Lives

Don't ignore persistent digestive symptoms like persistent vomiting, abdominal pain, jaundice, altered bowel habits, rectal bleeding and weight loss – schedule a consultation today.

Dr. Raiz Hussain

MBBS, FCPS (Pulmonary Medicine)

Consultant Pulmonologist | Sukkur, Sindh

As a pulmonologist, my role is detecting lung cancer early and guiding patients to oncologists for timely, life-saving treatment.

How I Help Diagnose Lung Cancer

1. Advanced Imaging & Scans:

  • CT Scans & X-rays: Identify suspicious lung nodules or masses
  • PET-CT: Assess if cancer has spread

2. Minimally Invasive Procedures:

  • Bronchoscopy: Examine airways and collect tissue samples (biopsy) to confirm cancer
  • EBUS (Endobronchial Ultrasound): Safely biopsy lymph nodes near the lungs to stage cancer
  • Thoracentesis: Analyse fluid around the lungs for cancer cells

3. Early Referral to Oncologists:

  • If cancer is confirmed, I promptly refer you to a medical oncologist (cancer specialist) to discuss chemotherapy, targeted therapy, or immunotherapy

Why Early Diagnosis Matters

Lung cancer often causes no symptoms until advanced stages. My role is to:

  • Investigate warning signs like persistent cough, blood in sputum, chest pain, shortness of breath, or unexplained weight loss
  • Use precise tests to diagnose cancer quickly
  • Connect you to specialists for personalized treatment

Working Together for Better Outcomes

  • Team Approach: I collaborate with oncologists, radiologists, and surgeons to ensure seamless care
  • Your Next Steps: If diagnosed, I explain your results clearly and guide you to trusted cancer experts

Early Detection Saves Lives

Don't ignore lingering respiratory symptoms – schedule a consultation today.

Dr. Samina Mansoor

MBBS, FCPS (General Surgery), FACS (USA)

Fellowship in Breast Oncoplastic Surgery (Malaysia)

Consultant Breast Oncoplastic Surgeon | Hyderabad

As a breast oncoplastic surgeon, I specialize in treating breast cancer with precision, combining tumour removal and aesthetic reconstruction, while coordinating care with oncologists for holistic treatment.

How I Help Diagnose & Treat Breast Cancer

1. Early Detection:

  • Core needle Biopsy & Imaging: Screening and diagnostic core needle biopsy with or without image guidance
  • Use ultrasound, mammography, or MRI to identify tumours

2. Surgical Expertise:

  • Breast-Conserving Surgery (Lumpectomy): Remove cancer while preserving breast shape
  • Mastectomy: Remove the breast tissue, when necessary, followed by immediate reconstruction for natural aesthetics
  • Oncoplastic Techniques: Reshape the breast during surgery to balance cancer removal with cosmetic outcomes
  • Preserving breast and breast shape

3. Lymph Node Assessment:

  • Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy: Check if cancer has spread to lymph nodes to guide further treatment
  • Axillary clearance to remove cancer involve lymph nodes

Teamwork for Complete Care

  • Referral to Medical Oncologists: Pre- and Post-surgery, I coordinate with Medical Oncologist (Cancer specialist) to plan chemotherapy, hormone therapy, or immunotherapy
  • Personalized Follow-Up: Monitor recovery and collaborate with Medical Oncologist (Cancer specialist) for adjuvant therapies if needed

Why Choose Oncoplastic Surgery?

  • Dual Focus: Prioritize cancer eradication and preserving your confidence through reconstruction
  • Early Action: Treating breast cancer at initial stages improves survival and reduces aggressive interventions

Early Detection Saves Lives

If you notice a lump/mass or breast swelling, skin changes, or nipple discharge, seek expert care without delay.
